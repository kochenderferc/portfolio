Computer Vision · Accessibility

Interpreting American Sign Language

Chapman University · with Natalie Paradiso · advised by Dr. Kendra Day · Dec 2025 – Jan 2026

A literature review of assistive technologies for interpreting American Sign Language (ASL). With Natalie Paradiso, I surveyed how current systems attempt to recognize ASL and where they fall short by comparing computer-vision approaches against wearable, sensor-based systems, and examining the human factors that decide whether a technology actually gets adopted.

What the review covers

  • Computer-vision models (deep learning, image fusion, keypoint tracking): accurate in controlled settings but brittle to signer variability, occlusion, and real-world noise
  • Wearable, sensor-based systems (smart gloves, motion-tracking): precise motion capture, but constrained by comfort, scalability, and social acceptability
  • Human factors: why designing with Deaf communities is essential to adoption, not an afterthought
  • Literature gaps: the lack of standardized, diverse, representative ASL datasets and consistent benchmarks
