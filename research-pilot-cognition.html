Cognitive Science · Aviation

Reaction Time in Autopilot vs. Manual Flight

Chapman University · LUCID Lab, Crean College · 2025 – Present

This is my independent research project in the LUCID Lab (Laboratory for Understanding Consciousness, Intentions, and Decision-Making). I'm studying how pilots' reaction times change between two modes of flying: when the aircraft is on autopilot versus when the pilot is flying manually, under different weather conditions.

Background

  • Advancements in aviation (autopilot, digital flight displays) reduce pilot workload and improve efficiency, but risk impacting attention, situational awareness, and decision-making
  • 60–80% of aircraft accidents are caused by human error

Goals
